Integral: The area under a curve.
Riemann Sum: A way of approximating the area under a curve by using rectangles of a set width. As the width gets smaller (or number of rectangles are increased) the area approaches the actual integral.
Left Endpoint Sum: A Riemann sum where the rectangles touch the function at the top left corner of the rectangle.
Right Endpoint Sum: A Riemann sum where the rectangles touch the function at the top right corner of the rectangle.
Midpoint Sum: A Riemann sum where the rectangles touch the function at the middle of the rectangle. Generally more accurate than left and right methods.
Trapezoidal Approximation: An approximation method which uses trapezoids instead of rectangles. Also equal to the average of the left and right Riemann sums.
Simpson's Rule: An approximation method which uses parabola topped rectangles instead of normal flat topped rectangles. The parabolas are graphed using an interpolation of three points (left bound, midpoint, and right bound).
